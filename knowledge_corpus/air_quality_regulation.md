# Regional Air Quality Regulation

Urban administrations should issue public advisories when PM2.5 exceeds moderate health thresholds and encourage reduced outdoor exertion near dense traffic corridors. Sensitive groups, including children, older adults, and people with asthma or cardiovascular conditions, should receive heightened warnings before pollution reaches acute emergency levels.

Short-term mitigation policies include dynamic traffic management, promotion of off-peak commuting, and temporary restrictions on construction dust. Public dashboards should translate pollutant measurements into plain-language risk bands and clearly state what behaviors are recommended for the current category.
