# Public Health Advisory for Outdoor Activity

Outdoor activity planning should consider particulate pollution, heat stress, humidity, and ultraviolet exposure together rather than in isolation. Early morning and late evening periods often provide the lowest combined exposure in dense urban environments, especially when road traffic is lighter.

When PM2.5 is elevated, residents should favor shorter trips, lower-intensity exercise, and routes away from major intersections. If UV levels are high, midday exposure should be limited and hydration, shade, and sun protection should be prioritized even when air quality is acceptable.
