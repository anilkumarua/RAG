# Sustainable Urban Mobility Guidance

Low-emission commuting recommendations should balance pollution avoidance with transport efficiency. Cycling and walking are preferred when air quality is good to moderate, but route selection matters: green corridors, side streets, and transit-first corridors generally reduce direct exposure compared with arterial roads.

For higher pollution periods, commuters should shift travel earlier or later when feasible, use public transport for long roadside stretches, and monitor wind conditions that may influence street-level pollutant dispersion. Smart-city systems should convert these patterns into specific timing advice so residents can make confident decisions.
